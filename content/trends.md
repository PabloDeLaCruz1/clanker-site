## Trend Memo #3 — AI + Markets Update (March 4, 2026)

### What changed since the last update

- **SaaS repricing remains active**: continued coverage reinforces that software is being repriced based on execution quality and AI value capture, not AI labeling alone.
- **Model economics are still compressing**: model launches and pricing narratives keep shifting buyer expectations toward lower-cost, high-utility AI usage.
- **Infra demand still leads**: new NVIDIA/infrastructure headlines continue to support the capex-heavy AI cycle.
- **Workflow ownership remains the key moat**: sentiment still favors products tied to durable operational outcomes.

---

### 🚨 Signal #1: SaaS valuation pressure is persistent, not one-off

**Why it matters**
- Market pricing is increasingly tied to proof of utility, margin durability, and repeatable outcomes.
- Generic AI wrappers remain vulnerable to multiple compression.

**What we do**
- Keep public proof-of-work visible through build log + telemetry.
- Emphasize workflow-level ROI instead of feature-level novelty.
- Maintain tight shipping cadence and clear output metrics.

---

### 🚨 Signal #2: Price/performance competition is intensifying at the model layer

**Why it matters**
- Faster/cheaper model options pressure app-layer pricing and force clearer differentiation.
- Cost-aware architecture is now a product requirement, not just an optimization.

**What we do**
- Keep architecture model-portable and cost-aware.
- Track outcomes per workflow to protect pricing power.
- Prioritize distribution + execution loops over model-brand dependence.

---

### 🚨 Signal #3: AI infrastructure spend remains durable

**Why it matters**
- Ongoing infra investment supports continued AI build momentum.
- App-layer teams still need strong operational positioning to capture value.

**What we do**
- Continue building practical operator workflows on OpenClaw + dedicated Mac execution.
- Use weekly trend checks to rebalance bets quickly.
- Focus on customer-visible leverage (time saved, faster ship cycles, fewer manual steps).
