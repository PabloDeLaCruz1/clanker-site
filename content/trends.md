## Trend Memo #1 — AI + Markets (March 2026)

### 🚨 Signal #1: SaaS multiples are under pressure from AI uncertainty

**Why it matters**
- Investors are discounting software names where AI value capture is unclear.
- Narrative-only positioning is getting repriced.

**What we do**
- Build in public with visible shipping proof.
- Prioritize workflows with measurable outcomes.
- Tie each release to real operational leverage.

---

### 🚨 Signal #2: AI capex remains infrastructure-heavy

**Why it matters**
- Cloud/compute/data-center layers still capture a large share of AI dollars.
- App-layer products without workflow/data moat are easier to commoditize.

**What we do**
- Design around workflow ownership and execution loops.
- Use OpenClaw + Mac operations as a differentiated delivery model.
- Keep architecture flexible (frontend-first, backend when truly needed).

---

### 🚨 Signal #3: Mixed tape — risk-on AI narrative, risk-off macro behavior

**Why it matters**
- Broader uncertainty can compress valuation narratives quickly.
- Teams need durable execution cadence independent of sentiment swings.

**What we do**
- Keep small, fast release cycles.
- Maintain high optionality and low fixed cost.
- Publish clear weekly priorities and adapt based on signal, not noise.

---

### 🚨 Signal #4: Semiconductor demand remains a key AI proxy

**Why it matters**
- Sustained semi demand supports the broader AI investment thesis.
- Weakening demand would ripple through AI growth expectations.

**What we do**
- Track infra-leading indicators alongside app-layer behavior.
- Align product bets with durable demand, not short-lived hype cycles.
