## Trend Memo #1 — AI + Markets (March 2026)

### 1) Signal: SaaS multiples are under pressure from AI uncertainty

- Investors are discounting software names where AI value capture is unclear.
- “Wait-and-see” sentiment is rising for app-layer businesses without a defensible moat.

### 2) Why it matters

- Narrative-only AI positioning is getting repriced.
- Markets are rewarding clear execution, distribution, and measurable outcomes over feature hype.

### 3) What we do

- Build in public with proof-of-work and visible shipping cadence.
- Prioritize workflows that produce measurable value, not generic wrappers.
- Tie each release to real user outcomes and operational leverage.

### 1) Signal: AI capex gravity remains infrastructure-heavy

- Cloud/compute/data-center spend still captures a large share of AI dollars.
- Application-layer winners need stronger differentiation to defend margins.

### 2) Why it matters

- If infra captures most upside, app products without unique data/workflow access get commoditized fast.

### 3) What we do

- Design around workflow ownership and execution loops.
- Use OpenClaw + Mac operations as a differentiated delivery model.
- Keep architecture flexible: frontend-first now, backend when required by secrets/jobs/auth.
