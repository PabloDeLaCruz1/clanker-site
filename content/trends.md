## Trend Memo #2 — AI + Markets Update (March 3, 2026)

### What changed since last memo

- **SaaS repricing narrative strengthened**: more coverage now frames SaaS as being valued differently (not dead), with winners expected to prove AI margin durability.
- **Cost pressure shifted to model economics**: major model updates are increasingly framed in cost/performance terms, not just benchmark hype.
- **Infra demand signal remains durable**: NVIDIA earnings cycle and infra-deal coverage continue to reinforce compute/networking capex strength.
- **Portfolio rotation still selective**: headlines suggest more discrimination between workflow-owned products and generic wrappers.

---

### 🚨 Signal #1: SaaS isn’t “dead,” but valuation discipline is increasing

**Why it matters**
- Market tone is separating profitable, defensible AI workflows from feature-level AI add-ons.
- Revenue quality and execution signal matter more than AI labeling.

**What we do**
- Keep shipping proof visible (build log, telemetry, demos).
- Prioritize measurable workflow outcomes over broad feature claims.
- Position around execution loop ownership, not generic tooling.

---

### 🚨 Signal #2: Model competition is becoming price/performance competition

**Why it matters**
- Lower-cost model tiers raise expectations for app efficiency and pricing pressure.
- Advantage shifts to products with strong distribution + workflow lock-in.

**What we do**
- Design for model portability and cost-aware architecture.
- Keep frontend-first speed while preserving backend optionality when needed.
- Track economics per workflow, not just model capability.

---

### 🚨 Signal #3: AI infrastructure spending remains resilient

**Why it matters**
- Continued infra strength supports medium-term AI build momentum.
- App-layer builders still need differentiated value capture to avoid margin compression.

**What we do**
- Build app-layer workflows tied to real operator pain.
- Use OpenClaw + dedicated Mac execution as a practical differentiation layer.
- Maintain weekly signal reviews to adjust bets quickly.
